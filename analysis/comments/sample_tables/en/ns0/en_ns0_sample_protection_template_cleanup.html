'''Sarong Party Girl''' (also known as '''SPG''') is a derogatory term used in [[Singapore]] and (to a lesser extent) in Peninsular [[Malaysia]].

It describes a local, solely [[Asian people|Asian woman]] (''e.g.'', a [[Chinese people|Chinese]] or [[Malays (ethnic group)|Malay]] girl) who usually dresses and behaves in a provocative manner, and who exclusively dates and prefers [[White people|white men]]. This can be considered a variant of the [[Pinkerton Syndrome]]. The Sarong Party Girl stereotype was popularised by a series of humorous books by [[Jim Aitchison]] in the 1990s, offering a satirical portrayal of the SPG and related aspects of Singaporean culture.

The '''Young America Movement''' was [[United States|an American ]] political and cultural attitude in the mid-nineteenth century. Inspired by [[Europe|European]] reform movements of the 1830s (see [[Young Italy]], [[Young Hegelians]]), the American group was formed as a political organization in 1845 by [[Edwin de Leon]] and [[George Henry Evans|George H. Evans]]. It advocated free trade, social reform, expansion southward into the territories, and support for [[Republicanism|republican, anti-aristocratic]] movements abroad. It became a faction in the Democratic Party in the 1850s. Sen. [[Stephen A. Douglas]] promoted its nationalistic program in an unsuccessful effort to compromise sectional differences.
